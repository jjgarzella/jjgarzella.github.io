There's a lot of great advice about how to apply for the NSF GRFP. 
[Here](https://www.kvasquez.me/resources/NSF.html)
(see also 
[here](https://caffeinatedconfidence.com/2017/09/01/all-about-the-nsf-grfp/))
is one place where you can find a rundown of the scholarship. 
You'll get a good idea of what you have to do, and a few strategies 
for how to do it. 

One important decision for an applicant to make is to choose when to apply.
The NSF GRFP is open to senior undergrads, as well as first or second year
grad students. 
However, a graduate student can only apply one out of their two first years;
they must choose to apply as a 1st year or a 2nd year.
If you're going to apply, it's a good idea to apply as an undegrad, but then 
you must choose whether to apply as a first year or a second year.

### How it went

The biggest problem with the "best of both worlds" approach was that it became really de-motivating.
Specifically, when I was applying, if anything bad happened, I had a crutch: "oh, I'll just apply next year".
That made it a lot easier to not work on my essays when I should have. 
Because I wasn't fully committed, I wasn't sold out.
